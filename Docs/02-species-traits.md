# Mushroom Species and Trait Taxonomy from Nya Svampboken

## Overview

This document identifies the common mushroom species from the Swedish field guide "Nya Svampboken" by Pelle Holmberg and Håkan Marklund, focusing on species suitable for an identification system. The guide emphasizes common edible mushrooms and their poisonous look-alikes, making it ideal for creating a balanced, safety-focused dataset.

## Trait Taxonomy

The following standardized traits will be used for all mushroom species in the dataset:

### Cap (Hatt)
- **Shape:** flat, convex, hemispherical, conical, umbonate, irregular
- **Color:** white, cream, yellow, orange, brown, red, purple, gray, black, multicolor
- **Surface Texture:** smooth, slightly wrinkled, cracked, scaly, sticky, matte, shiny
- **Size Range:** small (1-3 cm), medium (3-8 cm), large (8-15 cm), very large (>15 cm)
- **Margin:** smooth, wavy, grooved, torn, rolled, inrolled

### Gills (Lameller)
- **Attachment:** free, adnate (attached flat), sinuate (notched), decurrent (running down stem), unequal (mixed sizes)
- **Density:** crowded, moderately spaced, distant
- **Color:** white, cream, pale yellow, pink, red, orange, brown, gray, black, variable/mixed
- **Edge:** smooth, serrated, irregular

### Stem (Stam)
- **Shape:** cylindrical, bulbous, tapered, curved, equal thickness, widening downward
- **Color:** white, cream, yellow, orange, brown, red, purple, gray
- **Surface:** smooth, ridged, fibrous, scaly, powdery
- **Hollow or Solid:** solid, stuffed, hollow
- **Root:** rooted (with club-like base), bulbous, absent, distinct mycelium
- **Ring/Veil Remnants:** present (leaving ring on stem), absent

### Flesh (Kött)
- **Color:** white, cream, yellow, orange, pink, red, brown, gray
- **Texture:** firm, soft, brittle, waxy, corky, dry
- **Change on Cutting:** no change, bruises blue, bruises red/pink, bruises black, stains yellow/orange
- **Smell:** not distinctive, mild mushroom, pleasant, unpleasant, acrid, fishy, phenolic

### Habitat (Växtplats)
- **Substrate Type:** soil (hardwood forest), soil (coniferous forest), soil (mixed forest), soil (heath/moorland), soil (grass/lawn), wood (fallen logs), wood (stumps), wood (live trees), wood (hardwood), wood (conifer), dung, moss, leaf litter
- **Associated Trees/Plants:** oak, beech, birch, aspen, pine, spruce, fir, mixed, none (meadow/heath)
- **Elevation/Region:** lowland, mid-elevation, mountain, coastal, widespread

### Season
- Spring (March-May)
- Early Summer (June-July)
- Late Summer (July-August)
- Autumn (September-October)
- Late Autumn (October-November)
- Winter (December-February)
- Year-round

### Growth Pattern
- Solitary (single fruiting bodies)
- Scattered (few, dispersed)
- Gregarious (groups, not touching)
- Clustered (groups, touching at base)
- Fairy rings (circular patterns)
- Caespitose (clustered on same point)

## Selected Species Dataset (20 Common Swedish Species)

### Edible Species (Ätliga svampar)

#### 1. **Chanterelle** | Kantarell
- **Swedish:** Kantarell
- **English:** Chanterelle, Golden Chanterelle
- **Scientific name:** *Cantharellus cibarius*
- **Cap:** Yellow-orange, funnel-shaped, decurrent ridges (not true gills), 3-7 cm
- **Gills:** Pale yellow, ridged, thick, distant, decurrent
- **Stem:** Yellow, solid, cylindrical, 3-5 cm tall
- **Flesh:** Pale yellow, firm, pleasant fruity smell
- **Habitat:** Hardwood and coniferous forests, especially with oak, birch, spruce
- **Season:** July-September
- **Growth:** Scattered to gregarious
- **Look-alike:** False chanterelle (toxic) - has thinner ridges and hollow stem
- **Safety Note:** HIGH - Often confused with toxic false chanterelle

#### 2. **Porcini** | Karljohan
- **Swedish:** Karljohan, Stensopp
- **English:** Porcini, Penny Bun, Cep
- **Scientific name:** *Boletus edulis*
- **Cap:** Brown, convex to flat, 5-30 cm, smooth, slightly sticky when moist
- **Pores:** Yellow, turning green-blue when bruised, small, 3-4 mm diameter
- **Stem:** White with brown network pattern, bulbous base, 8-20 cm, solid
- **Flesh:** White, firm, no color change when cut
- **Habitat:** Coniferous forests (pine, spruce), sometimes birch
- **Season:** July-October
- **Growth:** Solitary to scattered
- **Look-alike:** Bitter bolete (*Caloboletus castaniceps*) - similar but bruises blue-green immediately
- **Safety Note:** MEDIUM - Similar species may be toxic

#### 3. **Morel** | Morkel
- **Swedish:** Morkel
- **English:** Morel, Common Morel
- **Scientific name:** *Morchella esculenta*
- **Cap:** Honeycomb-like pits, tan to brown, conical/ovoid, 4-8 cm tall
- **Pits:** Gray-brown, arranged in rows
- **Stem:** Pale, hollow, bulbous base, 3-5 cm tall
- **Flesh:** Hollow in both cap and stem
- **Habitat:** Hardwood forests (ash, elm, poplar), sometimes burned areas
- **Season:** April-May (spring)
- **Growth:** Scattered to gregarious
- **Look-alike:** False morel (*Gyromitra esculenta*) - brain-like ridges, attached at sides
- **Safety Note:** HIGH - False morels are toxic

#### 4. **Bay Bolete** | Brunsopp
- **Swedish:** Brunsopp, Flugsopp
- **English:** Bay Bolete, Iodine Bolete
- **Scientific name:** *Boletus badius*
- **Cap:** Dark brown to bay color, convex, 3-10 cm, matte
- **Pores:** Bright yellow, turning blue-green when bruised
- **Stem:** Yellow-orange at top, brown at base, solid, 4-8 cm
- **Flesh:** Yellow under cap skin, white deeper, blue bruising
- **Habitat:** Coniferous and mixed forests, usually with spruce or pine
- **Season:** July-October
- **Growth:** Scattered
- **Look-alike:** Bitter bolete - similar appearance, better bruising
- **Safety Note:** MEDIUM - Some similar species cause gastroenteritis

#### 5. **Honey Mushroom** | Hallon
- **Swedish:** Hallon, Honungsskivling
- **English:** Honey Mushroom, Honey Fungus
- **Scientific name:** *Armillaria mellea* (complex)
- **Cap:** Yellow-brown to brown, convex, 2-6 cm, may have dark scales
- **Gills:** Cream to pale brown, attached, decurrent, crowded
- **Stem:** Yellow above, brown below, with distinctive ring, 3-7 cm
- **Flesh:** White, fragrant, firm
- **Habitat:** Wood - logs, stumps, wounded trees (hardwood and conifer)
- **Season:** September-November
- **Growth:** Caespitose (clustered)
- **Look-alike:** Funeral bell (*Galerina marginata*) - lacks ring, poisonous
- **Safety Note:** HIGH - Toxic lookalikes; must be cooked before eating

#### 6. **Wood Ear** | Träöra
- **Swedish:** Träöra, Judasöra
- **English:** Wood Ear, Jelly Ear, Black Fungus
- **Scientific name:** *Auricularia auricula-judae*
- **Cap:** Dark brown to black, ear-shaped, gelatinous, 3-6 cm, whitish underneath
- **Gills/Ridges:** Veins rather than true gills, on underside
- **Stem:** Absent or very short
- **Flesh:** Gelatinous, rubber-like texture
- **Habitat:** Wood - usually elder or occasionally other hardwoods
- **Season:** Year-round, especially autumn/winter
- **Growth:** Scattered to clustered
- **Look-alike:** Jelly ear (*Tremella mesenterica*) - bright yellow, different texture
- **Safety Note:** LOW - Few toxic look-alikes

#### 7. **Puffball** | Rökslöjpa
- **Swedish:** Rökslöjpa, Kärnsvamp, Krustsvamp
- **English:** Puffball, Common Puffball, Gem-studded Puffball
- **Scientific name:** *Lycoperdon perlatum* and similar
- **Cap:** Rounded to pear-shaped, white with brown spines when young, 2-4 cm
- **Pores:** Single opening at top (not gills), releases spores
- **Stem:** Narrow attachment to substrate
- **Flesh:** White and firm when young, becomes dark and powdery with age
- **Habitat:** Soil - grasslands, meadows, heaths, sometimes forests
- **Season:** July-October
- **Growth:** Scattered to gregarious, sometimes in fairy rings
- **Look-alike:** Young death caps (*Amanita phalloides*) - different internal structure
- **Safety Note:** VERY HIGH - Must be eaten only when completely white inside; older specimens are inedible

#### 8. **Oyster Mushroom** | Ostronskivling
- **Swedish:** Ostronskivling, Älsksovling
- **English:** Oyster Mushroom, Oyster Fungus
- **Scientific name:** *Pleurotus species* (e.g., *Pleurotus ostreatus*)
- **Cap:** Gray to brown/white, fan or oyster-shaped, 5-15 cm, convex
- **Gills:** White to cream, decurrent, crowded, forked
- **Stem:** Off-center or lateral, white, short, solid
- **Flesh:** White, firm, pleasant mild smell
- **Habitat:** Wood - logs, dead trees (hardwood and conifer)
- **Season:** Year-round, especially September-May
- **Growth:** Caespitose
- **Look-alike:** Angel wings (*Pleurotus porrigens*) - very thin, almost transparent
- **Safety Note:** LOW - Few toxic look-alikes

### Poisonous/Toxic Species (Giftiga svampar)

#### 9. **Death Cap** | Dödskallesvamp
- **Swedish:** Dödskallesvamp, Flugsvamp
- **English:** Death Cap, Deathcap, Poison Cup
- **Scientific name:** *Amanita phalloides*
- **Cap:** White, cream, or pale olive-green, convex, 4-10 cm, smooth, shiny
- **Gills:** White, free, crowded
- **Stem:** White, hollow, with prominent white ring and cup-like base (volva), 8-15 cm
- **Flesh:** White, distinctive smell (like radish), bitter taste (spit out)
- **Habitat:** Mixed forests (oak, beech, chestnut - associated with trees), usually soil
- **Season:** August-October
- **Growth:** Solitary to scattered
- **Look-alike:** Young puffballs, young champignon, edible Amanitas
- **Toxicity:** EXTREMELY TOXIC - Contains amatoxins, lethal in small amounts
- **Safety Note:** CRITICAL - One of most dangerous mushrooms; responsible for ~90% mushroom deaths

#### 10. **Destroying Angel** | Änglsvamp
- **Swedish:** Änglsvamp, Destruktiv änglsvamp, Vit änglsvamp
- **English:** Destroying Angel, Death Angel, Destroying Angels
- **Scientific name:** *Amanita virosa* and similar species
- **Cap:** White or pale, convex, 5-10 cm, smooth
- **Gills:** White, free, crowded
- **Stem:** White, hollow, with ring and bulbous base with volva, 8-15 cm
- **Flesh:** White, mild smell, no distinctive odor
- **Habitat:** Hardwood forests (beech), soil
- **Season:** August-October
- **Growth:** Solitary
- **Look-alike:** Death cap (both highly toxic)
- **Toxicity:** EXTREMELY TOXIC - Contains amatoxins
- **Safety Note:** CRITICAL - Another deadly Amanita species

#### 11. **Fly Agaric** | Flugsvamp
- **Swedish:** Flugsvamp, Röd flugsvamp
- **English:** Fly Agaric, Fly Amanita, Toadstool
- **Scientific name:** *Amanita muscaria*
- **Cap:** Bright red to orange-red, convex to flat, 5-20 cm, with white spots/warts, smooth
- **Gills:** White, free, crowded
- **Stem:** White with ring and bulbous base with white volva, 8-15 cm, hollow
- **Flesh:** White, pleasant smell, poisonous
- **Habitat:** Coniferous and birch forests, mycorrhizal with trees
- **Season:** August-October
- **Growth:** Solitary to scattered
- **Look-alike:** Caesar's mushroom (*Amanita caesarea*) - orange-red cap, yellow gills (not found in Sweden)
- **Toxicity:** TOXIC - Contains ibotenic acid; causes neurological effects
- **Safety Note:** MEDIUM - Distinctive appearance but sometimes eaten in other cultures

#### 12. **Funeral Bell** | Små änglsvamp
- **Swedish:** Små änglsvamp, Liten änglsvamp
- **English:** Funeral Bell, Deadly Webcap, Poison Galerina
- **Scientific name:** *Galerina marginata*
- **Cap:** Brown, bell or conical, 1-3 cm, moist, sticky
- **Gills:** Pale brown, attached, crowded, decurrent
- **Stem:** Brown, dark below, 2-4 cm, no ring or very faint ring
- **Flesh:** Brown, thin, fragile
- **Habitat:** Wood - dead logs, decaying wood (hardwood and conifer)
- **Season:** Year-round, especially spring and autumn
- **Growth:** Scattered to gregarious
- **Look-alike:** Honey mushroom young fruitbodies (which have ring)
- **Toxicity:** TOXIC - Contains amatoxins (same as death cap)
- **Safety Note:** HIGH - Small but deadly; often on wood like edible species

#### 13. **False Chanterelle** | Falsk kantarell
- **Swedish:** Falsk kantarell, Träkantarell
- **English:** False Chanterelle, Mushroomgill, Orange Elf Cup
- **Scientific name:** *Hygrophorellus pseudoaurantiacus* / *Gerronema luteofolidum*
- **Cap:** Orange-yellow, funnel-shaped, 1-4 cm, thinner than true chanterelle
- **Ridges:** Thinner, more blade-like than true gills, false gills
- **Stem:** Thin, hollow, fragile, yellow-orange, 2-3 cm
- **Flesh:** Thin, orange, watery
- **Habitat:** Coniferous and hardwood forests, on soil
- **Season:** July-September
- **Growth:** Scattered to gregarious
- **Look-alike:** True chanterelle - larger, thicker, flesh-colored gills
- **Toxicity:** Possibly toxic, causes gastrointestinal issues
- **Safety Note:** VERY HIGH - Often mistaken for chanterelle; identification critical

#### 14. **Bitter Bolete** | Bitter karljohan
- **Swedish:** Bitter karljohan, Karljohanbror
- **English:** Bitter Bolete, Bitter Beechwood Sickener
- **Scientific name:** *Caloboletus castaniceps*
- **Cap:** Brown, convex to flat, 5-20 cm, smooth, sticky
- **Pores:** Yellow to orange, turning blue-green immediately when bruised
- **Stem:** Pale yellow at top, brown at base, bulbous, 5-15 cm
- **Flesh:** White with yellow tinge, bitter taste (spit out), immediate blue bruising
- **Habitat:** Hardwood forests (oak, beech), sometimes conifers
- **Season:** July-October
- **Growth:** Solitary
- **Look-alike:** Porcini bolete - larger, less bitter, network pattern on stem
- **Toxicity:** TOXIC - Causes severe gastrointestinal distress
- **Safety Note:** HIGH - Similar to edible boletes; taste test dangerous

#### 15. **Lepiota Species** | Giftskivling
- **Swedish:** Giftskivling, Rödbrun giftskivling, Liten giftskivling
- **English:** Lepiota, Toxic Lepiota, Deadly Lepiota
- **Scientific name:** *Lepiota helveola* / *Lepiota brunneoincarnata*
- **Cap:** Pale brown, bell-shaped, 2-4 cm, with fine radial grooves, scaly
- **Gills:** White, free, crowded, delicate
- **Stem:** White to pale, fragile, 2-4 cm, with fine ring, bulbous base
- **Flesh:** Thin, white, fragile, unpleasant smell
- **Habitat:** Soil - grasslands, meadows, sometimes forests
- **Season:** August-October
- **Growth:** Scattered
- **Look-alike:** Button mushrooms (*Agaricus* species) - larger, different gill attachment
- **Toxicity:** TOXIC - Amatoxins present
- **Safety Note:** HIGH - Small, easily overlooked, contains deadly toxins

#### 16. **Orange Milk Cap** | Smörkantarell
- **Swedish:** Smörkantarell, Krämsmörkantarell
- **English:** Orange Milk Cap, Saffron Milk Cap, Delicious Milk Cap
- **Scientific name:** *Lactarius deliciosus*
- **Cap:** Orange with darker rings/zones, funnel-shaped, 4-8 cm
- **Gills:** Orange, decurrent, crowded
- **Stem:** Orange, hollow, 3-5 cm
- **Flesh:** Orange, exudes orange "milk" when cut/bruised
- **Habitat:** Coniferous forests (pine, spruce), mycorrhizal
- **Season:** August-November
- **Growth:** Scattered to gregarious
- **Look-alike:** False milk cap (*Lactarius mitissimus*) - paler, different habitat

#### 17. **Chicken of the Woods** | Kejsarsvamp
- **Swedish:** Kejsarsvamp, Kejsarchanterelle
- **English:** Chicken of the Woods, Chicken Fungus, Sulphur Shelf
- **Scientific name:** *Laetiporus sulphureus* / *Laetiporus crinitus*
- **Cap:** Bright orange to yellow, shelf-like bracket, 10-40 cm, wavy margin
- **Pores:** Small yellow pores underneath (not gills), soft, turning orange-brown
- **Stem:** Absent (shelf mushroom)
- **Flesh:** Yellow, fibrous, thick
- **Habitat:** Wood - dead logs, wounded live trees (hardwood, especially oak)
- **Season:** May-November (especially summer)
- **Growth:** Shelf-like, clustered
- **Look-alike:** Other bracket fungi - distinguished by bright colors
- **Safety Note:** MEDIUM - Must be cooked thoroughly; some people have adverse reactions

#### 18. **Shaggy Inkcap** | Spindelskivling
- **Swedish:** Spindelskivling, Bläcksvamp, Scaly Inkcap
- **English:** Shaggy Inkcap, Lawyer's Wig, Ink Cap
- **Scientific name:** *Coprinellus comatus*
- **Cap:** White with brown scales, cylindrical to bell-shaped, 4-8 cm, fragile
- **Gills:** White, turning black and dissolving (inky), free, crowded
- **Stem:** White, hollow, fragile, with ring, 8-12 cm
- **Flesh:** White, fragile, delicate smell
- **Habitat:** Soil - disturbed areas, grasslands, gardens, roadsides
- **Season:** May-October
- **Growth:** Scattered to gregarious
- **Look-alike:** Other inkcap species (*Coprininus* species) - similar appearance
- **Safety Note:** MEDIUM - Must be eaten immediately after harvest (gills dissolve)

#### 19. **Parasol Mushroom** | Parasolsvamp
- **Swedish:** Parasolsvamp, Stor parasolsvamp
- **English:** Parasol Mushroom, Umbrella Mushroom, Tall Parasol
- **Scientific name:** *Macrolepiota procera*
- **Cap:** Pale brown with brown scales on white background, convex with central umbo, 8-15 cm
- **Gills:** White, free or nearly so, crowded
- **Stem:** Tall, pale brown, scaly pattern, prominent double ring, hollow, 15-25 cm
- **Flesh:** White, pleasant smell
- **Habitat:** Soil - grasslands, meadows, open woodlands, sandy areas
- **Season:** August-October
- **Growth:** Solitary to scattered
- **Look-alike:** Small parasol species, some poisonous lookalikes
- **Safety Note:** HIGH - Must not confuse with toxic Amanita species; ring present distinguishes from some false lookalikes

#### 20. **Meadow Mushroom** | Ängssvamp
- **Swedish:** Ängssvamp, Ängssvamp
- **English:** Meadow Mushroom, Field Mushroom, Meadow Button
- **Scientific name:** *Agaricus campestris*
- **Cap:** White to cream, convex, 4-8 cm, smooth, browning with age
- **Gills:** Pink when young, turning dark brown-black with age, free, crowded
- **Stem:** White, fragile, no ring, 2-4 cm
- **Flesh:** White, pleasant smell, turning slightly pink where bruised
- **Habitat:** Soil - grasslands, meadows, lawns
- **Season:** May-November (peak July-October)
- **Growth:** Solitary to scattered, often in fairy rings
- **Look-alike:** Button/cultivated mushroom (*Agaricus bisporus*), young Amanitas
- **Safety Note:** VERY HIGH - Can be confused with deadly Amanitas in early button stage; gills must be checked carefully

## Look-Alike Relationships (Critical for Safety)

```
EDIBLE → TOXIC LOOKALIKE
Chanterelle → False Chanterelle (inedible/toxic)
Porcini → Bitter Bolete (toxic)
Morel → False Morel (toxic)
Honey Mushroom → Funeral Bell (toxic - deadly amatoxins)
Puffball (young) → Young Death Cap (deadly)
Meadow Mushroom → Death Cap (deadly)
Parasol Mushroom → Some Amanita species (deadly)
Orange Milk Cap → Some Lactarius species (inedible)
```

## Dataset Characteristics

### By Identification Difficulty
- **Easy:** Fly Agaric, Wood Ear, Chicken of the Woods, Shaggy Inkcap
- **Moderate:** Chanterelle, Porcini, Honey Mushroom, Parasol, Orange Milk Cap
- **Difficult:** Morel vs. False Morel, Death Cap vs. young champignon, Bitter Bolete vs. Porcini
- **Very Difficult:** Funeral Bell vs. Honey Mushroom, similar Lactarius species

### Trait Variation
- **High Visual Variation:** Boletes, Agaricus species
- **Distinctive Appearance:** Fly Agaric, Wood Ear, Shaggy Inkcap
- **Habitat-Dependent:** Many species require habitat knowledge for confirmation
- **Season-Dependent:** All species have seasonal occurrence windows

## Notes for Dataset Construction

1. **Image Sources:**
   - Nya Svampboken book photos (if digitally available)
   - Swedish mushroom databases and field observation photos
   - Multiple angles and growth stages per species

2. **Trait Data:**
   - Use standardized trait values defined in taxonomy
   - Note variability (e.g., color ranges)
   - Include seasonal and habitat context

3. **Safety Emphasis:**
   - Highlight all lookalike relationships
   - Ensure toxic species are clearly marked
   - Include deadly species even if not target for identification

4. **Balancing:**
   - Similar number of images per species
   - Represent common variations in appearance
   - Include different growth stages where relevant
